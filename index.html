Part 1

Setting Up the Game

Complete the following steps in order before play begins. Skipping a step may result in an unequal deal or disorganized game.

⚠️ Important: Do not use a deck containing Jokers. Remove them before dealing — they have no defined rank in War and will cause confusion.
  1. 1

    Remove the Jokers

    Locate and remove both Joker cards from the deck. Set them aside — they are not used in War.

  2. 2

    Shuffle the deck

    Hold all 52 cards and shuffle them thoroughly at least three times to ensure a random distribution.

  3. 3

    Designate a dealer

    Either player may deal. Decide between yourselves — flip a card to break a tie if needed.

  4. 4

    Deal the cards face-down

    The dealer distributes all 52 cards one at a time, alternating between players (Player 1, Player 2, Player 1…), until each player holds exactly 26 cards. Do not look at your cards during dealing.

  5. 5

    Form your draw pile

    Each player stacks their 26 cards into a single face-down pile in front of them. This pile is called your draw pile.

    📌 Keep your draw pile face-down at all times. Looking at upcoming cards is not permitted.
Part 2

Playing a Round

A "round" consists of each player flipping one card. Repeat these steps continuously until the game ends.

  1. 6

    Flip your top card

    Simultaneously, each player takes the top card from their draw pile and places it face-up in the center of the table between both players.

  2. 7

    Compare the two cards

    Look at the rank of each face-up card and identify which is higher. Refer to the Card Ranks table in the Overview section above if needed.

    Award the cards

    The player whose card is higher wins the round. That player collects both face-up cards and places them face-down at the bottom of their own draw pile. The winner may add the cards in any order.

  4. 9

    Continue

    Begin the next round immediately. Repeat Steps 6–8 until a tie occurs or the game ends.

Part 3

Resolving a Tie — "War"

When both players flip cards of equal rank, a tie occurs. The tie-breaking procedure is called War. Follow these steps exactly.

  1. 10

    Leave the tied cards on the table

    Do not collect them yet. The two tied cards remain face-up in the center of the table.

  2. 11

    Each player deals three cards face-down

    From your draw pile, take the next three cards off the top and place them face-down on top of your tied card. These are played blind — neither player sees them.

    ⚠️ Insufficient cards: If you have fewer than four cards when War is declared, see the special rules below.
  3. 12

    Each player flips a fourth card face-up

    After placing the three face-down cards, each player flips one additional card face-up beside them. This is the new battle card.

  4. 13

    Compare the new battle cards

    The player with the higher face-up card wins all cards on the table — both tied cards, all six face-down cards, and the two new face-up cards (10 cards total).

    📌 If the two battle cards also tie, repeat the entire War procedure immediately from Step 11.

Insufficient Cards During War

Apply whichever scenario fits your situation:

Scenario A — One card remaining

If a player has only one card left when War is declared, that player flips their single remaining card as the battle card. No face-down cards are played. Compare the two battle cards normally.

Scenario B — Runs out mid-War

If a player cannot place any cards at all, that player loses the game immediately. They have run out of cards and can no longer participate.

Part 4

Ending the Game

  1. 14

    Standard win condition

    The game ends when one player collects all 52 cards. That player is the winner.

  2. 15

    Opponent runs out of cards

    The game also ends the moment one player's draw pile is completely empty and they cannot flip a card to begin a new round. The player who still holds cards wins.

    📌 Games of War can last 15–45+ minutes. To set a time limit, agree on a duration before starting — the player holding more cards when time expires wins.

Quick-Reference Checklist

Use this checklist once you're familiar with the full rules.

  • Remove Jokers. Shuffle the 52-card deck.
  • Deal 26 cards face-down to each player.
  • Form face-down draw piles.
  • Both players flip their top card simultaneously each round.
  • Higher card wins both cards; winner places them at the bottom of their pile.
  • On a tie: place 3 cards face-down, then flip a 4th face-up as the battle card.
  • Higher battle card wins all 10 cards from a War round.
  • First player to collect all 52 cards wins.
